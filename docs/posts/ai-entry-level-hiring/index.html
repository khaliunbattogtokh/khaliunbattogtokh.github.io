Did ChatGPT Reshape Entry-Level Job Postings?

Examining whether the launch of generative AI reduced entry-level job postings in AI-exposed occupations.

The Question

In November 2022, OpenAI launched ChatGPT, and within weeks it became the fastest-growing consumer application in history. Employers began experimenting with AI tools for drafting, coding, data processing, and customer interaction — tasks that had, until recently, been the domain of entry-level workers.

This raises a narrow but consequential question: did ChatGPT’s launch reduce the share of job postings that are entry-level in occupations more exposed to AI?

Why job postings? Job postings capture employer intent before a hire is made. If a firm knows AI can perform certain tasks faster and at lower cost, the first observable response is a change in who they look for — before any layoff or headcount reduction shows up in employment data. This makes postings a leading indicator of AI’s effect on labor demand composition.

Data

The analysis uses Lightcast job postings data from 2020 to 2024, covering approximately 92% of all US job postings. The sample covers 733 five-digit SOC occupations across 63 months (January 2020 – March 2025), yielding approximately 45,000 occupation × month observations.

A posting is classified as entry-level if it requires 2 years or less of experience (or has a Junior seniority label with no experience listed) and does not require graduate education.

AI exposure is measured two ways:

  • gpt4_beta (Eloundou et al., 2023): the share of an occupation’s tasks for which GPT-4 can provide meaningful assistance, based on O*NET task descriptions
  • observed_exposure (Anthropic Economic Index): derived from actual Claude usage patterns by occupation, reflecting revealed AI adoption

The two measures correlate at r = 0.638 (Spearman ρ = 0.699).

What the Data Show

Entry-level postings fell hardest in high-AI occupations

In software development (GPT-4 exposure = 0.94), postings by experience bucket tell a clear story. Indexed to October 2022 = 1:

  • Entry-level (0–3 YOE) fell to 56% of baseline
  • Mid-level (4–6 YOE) fell to 66%
  • Senior (7+ YOE) fell to 81%

Entry-level postings fell 2.4× harder than senior postings. The same pattern holds in Financial and Investment Analysis: senior postings reached 110% of their October 2022 level while entry-level and mid-level remain at 78%.

Overall entry-level postings have not recovered

Across all occupations, postings requiring 0–3 YOE and 4–6 YOE declined after ChatGPT’s launch and have not recovered. Only the highest-experience group (11+ YOE) has grown substantially. This is not just a tech sector story — it holds economy-wide.

The decline is concentrated in automating occupations

Splitting occupations by whether their AI exposure is primarily automating (AI replaces tasks) or augmenting (AI complements workers) reveals stark heterogeneity. In automating occupations, higher-exposure quintiles fall furthest after October 2022. In augmenting occupations, the pattern reverses — higher-exposure occupations hold up better or grow. The aggregate decline in entry-level hiring is not uniform; it is driven by the automating end of the AI exposure spectrum.

Event study

The event study tests whether the divergence began precisely at ChatGPT’s launch or whether groups were already trending apart beforehand. Pre-treatment coefficients are noisy but centered near zero — high- and low-exposure occupations were on parallel trajectories before October 2022. Post-treatment coefficients turn negative immediately after launch and remain negative.

Regression Results

To move beyond descriptive comparisons, I estimate a difference-in-differences model with two-way fixed effects:

\[\text{EntryShare}_{it} = \beta \cdot (\text{Post}_t \times \text{Exposure}_i) + \alpha_i + \gamma_t + \varepsilon_{it}\]

where occupation fixed effects absorb time-invariant differences and time fixed effects absorb macro shocks common to all occupations. The model is estimated by WLS weighted by 2021 posting shares, with standard errors clustered by occupation.

Specification Treatment Coef. SE p-value 95% CI
(1) Main DiD gpt4_beta −1.729 0.898 0.054 [−3.49, 0.03]
(2) + rate sensitivity gpt4_beta −1.926 0.824 0.019 [−3.54, −0.31]
(3) Main DiD observed_exposure −2.243 0.921 0.015 [−4.05, −0.44]
(4) + rate sensitivity observed_exposure −2.426 0.889 0.006 [−4.17, −0.68]

WLS with 2021 posting weights, occupation + year-month FEs, SEs clustered by occupation. *p<0.10, **p<0.05, ***p<0.01.

All four specifications are negative; three of four are significant at the 5% level. Adding a rate sensitivity control — each occupation’s pre-period sensitivity to Fed funds rate changes — increases the magnitude, because AI-exposed occupations are less rate-sensitive. Disentangling the rate cycle makes the AI signal cleaner, not weaker.

Benchmark: Software Developers (gpt4_beta = 0.94) versus Truck Drivers (gpt4_beta = 0.04) represent a 0.90-unit gap. Spec 2 implies a −1.73 percentage point differential decline in entry-level share after ChatGPT’s launch.

Conclusion

This analysis documents four findings:

  1. Entry-level postings fell 2.4× harder than senior in software developer job postings
  2. Overall entry-level postings have not recovered to pre-2022 levels, while high-experience postings grew
  3. High-AI occupations saw a ≈ −2.4 pp decline in entry-level share post-ChatGPT, significant at 5% after controlling for rate sensitivity
  4. The decline is concentrated in automating occupations; augmenting occupations show a different pattern

These findings do not settle the question of causality. The post-2022 period brought overlapping shocks — the rate cycle, tech sector correction, and post-pandemic normalization. But the consistency across exposure measures, specifications, and occupation types points in the same direction.

How this plays out over a longer horizon remains open. Prior technology transitions have ultimately created new categories of work even as they displaced old ones. Whether generative AI follows that pattern, or compresses the entry tier in ways that are more persistent, is a question the data will answer over time.
